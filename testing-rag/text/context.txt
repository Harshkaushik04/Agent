Beach („Éì„Éº„ÉÅ, Bƒ´chi) is the name given to "Resort Hotel Tama Pacific" by Takeru Danma (Hatter), as well as the name of one of the groups in Alice in Borderland. It is a "utopia" founded by Hatter with the help of his friend Morizono Aguni to bring hope to the players by providing them with a haven filled with luxuries and comforts.
"WELCOME!! To the ideal utopia, the Beach!"‚Äî Takeru Danma, Chapter 15
The Beach is governed by a system of ranks. Each resident of the Beach is assigned a number that indicates their status within the organization, and the lower the number, the higher the rank. Those with the highest ranks (1 to 9) form the Executives, and No. 1 occupies the position of leader. As people die, a person's rank increases. People who contribute new or many cards may be promoted.
Within the Beach Executives, there are two factions that hold influence. The Idealist/Cult Sect was led by Hatter; important members were Kuzuryu, Ann, Mira, and Mahiru. The Martial Sect was led by Aguni; important members were Niragi and "Last Boss". They also have control over most of the firearms. However, the Martial Sect functions as a method for Aguni, secretly allied with Hatter, to keep the most violent residents contained. There are also other groups, such as the Gasoline Provisional Team, which Tatta is a part of.
You are free to live your life as you wish.
Death to all traitors. All cards must be given to the Top 1 resident, and no one may leave once admitted to the Beach. Going against this is considered "traitorous".
The Idealists follow Hatter's ideology. They establish the rules of the Beach and decide who participates in the games. Secretly, two members of this faction are Citizens of the Borderland who later take part in the Face Card games.
The Militants follow Aguni's ideology. They maintain control of the firearms and are willing to enforce the rules and eliminate those considered traitors to the Beach. Aguni, who is Hatter's close friend, leads this group to prevent the most violent residents, such as Niragi, from acting without restraint.
Unknown to the players, there are four infiltrators at the Beach. Asahi and Momoka are Dealers ordered to infiltrate the Beach and eventually initiate the Ten of Hearts game. Meanwhile, Kuzuryu and Mira are both Citizens who infiltrate the Beach out of curiosity.
After the death of the Hatter, the Beach becomes the game zone of the "Witch Hunt" game, and Aguni and his men murder most of the Beach residents. Once the truth behind the game and Aguni's intentions is revealed, a fire started by Niragi destroys the Beach after the survivors clear the game. However, Chishiya and Kuina recover the Ten of Hearts card just before the Beach burns down completely.

Beach („Éì„Éº„ÉÅ, Bƒ´chi) is the name given to the "Seaside Paradise Tokyo" hotel by Takeru Danma (Hatter), as well as the name of one of the groups in the Alice in Borderland series. It was created by Hatter with the help of his friend, Morizono Aguni. It is meant to be a "utopia", a safe haven where players have hope, as they unite to find all of the cards and bring everyone back home, one by one, starting with Hatter himself.
Seaside Paradise Tokyo hotel („Éì„Éº„ÉÅ bii chi)
The Beach is located in a large hotel "Seaside Paradise Tokyo". Members use fuels to generate electricity and rainwater as a water supply, so that everyone can enjoy their time. The transportation division provides Beach members with cars, which allows them to travel to game arenas more easily.
The Beach is governed by a rank system. Each resident of the Beach is assigned a number that indicates their status within the organization, the lower the number, the higher the rank, with #1 being the boss and ranks 2-9 forming the executive members. From the inside, the top of the organization is divided into two factions: idealists and militants. Idealists follow Hatter believing in his theory wholeheartedly, while militants listen to Aguni and control all firearms, which allows them to dispose of traitors.
While at the Beach, most people try to "escape from the reality" (as noted by Usagi): they drink cocktails, dance, party by the pool, sunbathe, and so on. The deadly games are perceived as another fun activity, and the players are excited when the evening comes. They cheer for Hatter who gives encouraging speeches and share his hope to eventually leave the Borderland by collecting all playing cards.
You must always wear swimsuits at the Beach, because you cannot hide firearms in a swimsuit, which makes it safe for everyone. Players are free to drink, take illegal substances and have sex, however, all the door locks are damaged with super glue, so nobody can hide anything.
All playing cards belong to the Beach. Those who have contributed more cards are promoted to a higher rank.
